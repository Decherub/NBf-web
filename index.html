Our Mission

With an irrevocable commitment to indigenous book development, anchored in the conviction that books build the nation, the NBF has as its mission the development of a vibrant indigenous book industry in Nigeria. The Foundation adopts a holistic approach to national book development, to ensure that all components of the book chain the author, the publisher, the printer, the bookseller, the librarian, the producer/importer of raw materials for book production, et cetera function maximally, and that the State provides a conducive environment for the book to flourish.

About the Book

His Majesty Eze Professor Chukwuemeka Ike was born to Mazi Charles Chinwuba Ike and Mrs. Dinah Mgbeke Ike (nee Ezeani) of Ikelionwu Ruling House, Ndikelionwu, in present day Orumba North LGA, Anambra State, Nigeria on Tuesday 28 April 1931,nkwo market day. He attended Government College, Umuahia (1945-1950) and University College, Ibadan (1951-1955), from where he obtained the B.A. (London) degree in 1955. In January 1967, he obtained the M.A. from Stanford University (USA). His Majesty had the privilege of teaching at primary, secondary, teacher-training, and university levels. From teaching in secondary school, he joined the senior administrative staff of University College Ibadan (1957-1960). In October 1960, he joined the pioneer staff of University of Nigeria, Nsukka, as Deputy Registrar, becoming the first Nigerian Registrar of the University in March 1963. At the end of the Nigeria/Biafra war in January 1970, the Administrator of East Central State, His Excellency Ukpabi Asika, appointed him Chairman of the Planning and Management Committee of the University, responsible for reopening the war battered University and Managing its affairs. In that capacity, he functioned as interim Chief Executive of the University, combining the role of Vice-Chancellor and Chairman of the Governing Council of the University at a most trying period in the life of the University. With the appointment of a Vice Chancellor and the installation of a regular Governing Council of the University in late 1970, Ike retired from University service in 1971 and moved with his family to Ghana to assume the post of Registrar and Chief Executive of the West African Examinations Council (WAEC), the first Nigerian to hold the position. During his tenure, WAEC, cut its umbilical cord with the University of Cambridge, University of London, Royal Society Arts, and City and Guilds of London Institute, becoming an autonomous examinations council whose certificates received national and international recognition. Also during his tenure, the Republic of Liberia, with historical links with the USA rather than the UK, joined the Council, thereby increasing the number of Member Countries from 4 to 5. In appreciation of his service as Registrar, City and Guilds of London Institute conferred on him (in 1978) the Honorary Fellowship of the Institute ( which carries the status of an honorary doctorate of the University of London). He retired voluntarily from WAEC and the Nigerian public service on 1st July, 1979,at the age 48. (For the records, he had the distinction of being the first W.A.E.C Registrar authorized to sign certificates of the Council along with the Chairman who had before then been the sole signatory).

MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE

ORGANIZATION OF THE FOUNDATION

NBF's operations are generally under the guidance of the Board of Trustees and the National Advisory Council. The Board of Trustees comprises the President of the Foundation (who is the Chairman of the Board) and not more than ten eminent Nigerians of high repute appointed on merit to reflect the rich diversity if talent nationwide in matters pertaining to book development.
The Terms of Reference of the Board of Trustees include:
(a) The general superintendence of the policies, finances, and physical assets of the Foundation.
(b) To hold in trust for the Foundation the Nigerian Book Foundation Fund, in a bank or banks as may be determined by the Board.
(c) To invest any part of the Fund and mobilize, on a continuing basis, additional contributions and gifts from organizations and individuals.
The National Advisory Council offers advice to the Foundation on the Foundation's annual programme and activities and helps to identify projects/activities in which intervention by the Foundation would be desirable, in the interest of indigenous book development, and to prioritize them. It enables the NBF to respond with one voice on behalf of the Nigerian book community as and when necessary.

MAJOR ACTIVITIES AND PROJECTS

1. Nigeria International Book Fair:
The Annual National Book Week, inaugurated by the NBF in April 1994 and usually held in the second half of April, metamorphosed into the Nigeria International Book Fair (NIBF) in May 2002. The NBF is a founding Trustee of the Nigerian Book Fair Trust (NBFT) which organizes the fair, participating in its activities.
2. Book Data Centre:
The NBF Book Data Centre was established in 1995 to serve as a data bank on book related matters. The maiden edition of the DIRECTORY OF NIGERIAN BOOK DEVELOPMENT edited by Chukwuemeka Ike and published in 1998, was the first publication of directories of Nigerian published Authors, Publishing Houses, Book Printers, Book selling and Distribution Organizations, and Libraries. Inadequate funding has hampered the work of the centre.
3. Promotion of Reading:
Funded by Heinrich- Boll Foundation of Germany, the NBF operated pilot reading promotion projects (1996 and 1997) and a 3- year (1998-2000) programme to promote the reading habit among Nigerians. Operating through three Reading Promotion Units in Ibadan (West), Jos (North), and Ndikelionwu (East), the NBF organized 3-day holistic reading promotion activities in urban and rural centres in 18 States of the Federation between 1998 and 2000.
The NBF handed over the mobile Library set up at Ibadan as part of the programme to Oyo State Library Board in 2002 at the request of the Board, which transformed it into Bodija Community Library, Bodija, Ibadan. The NBF has the capacity to mount new reading promotion programmes as funds become available.
4. Book Aid Nigeria (BAN) Project:
As a follow up of the NBF/Heinrich Boll Foundation Reading Promotion Programme which expired in December 2000, the NBF lunched a Book Aid Nigeria (BAN) Project aimed at receiving free donations of a wide range of new books for distribution to public, institutional, community, and other libraries, organizations, and (in approved cases) individuals, to facilitate access to books. The first container of imported books for the project came from International Book Bank Inc, Baltimore, Macmillan, Longman Nigeria and University Press also donated books. A 3- year Project with Sabre Foundation, Massachusetts, U.SA will enable the NBF to receive and distribute two consignments of books from the U.S.A annually. A grant from the Education Tax Fund (ETF) facilitated the acquisition of an NBF warehouse for the project. The NBF welcomes more book donations and funds for the purchase of books, to meet the ever-growing demand for books for formal education and life-long education.
5.Sourcing and Dissemination of indigenous knowledge:
Through organized interviews with identified experts (literate and illiterate) in indigenous knowledge, the NBF has sourced hitherto unpublished information, which it transformed into reading materials for the reading public. Seven Igbo language booklets have been produced. Inadequate funds have held back quality publication of the booklets as well as expansion of the scope of the Project to other Nigerian language groups.

Our History.

In an attempt to find a lasting solution to a national book Crisis, a concerned writer,
Prof. Chukwuemeka Ike convened a meeting of writers.
publishers, and representatives of various professional groups in the book industry at Ibadan on
May 24, 1990. The meeting resolved to establish the Nigerian Book Development Forum (NBDF).
The forum elected Professor Ike as convener and appointed a task force with the following
membership to act on its behalf when necessary. *Professor Chukwuemeka Ike,
*The Hon. Dr. T.Akinola Aguda,
*Mr. B.O. Bolodeoku,
*Mr. E. AkinThomas,
*Mrs. Mabel D. Segun,
*Chief Nuhu Bature,
*Dr. Bode Sowande,
*Engr. Victor U. Nwankwo,
*Professor Theo Vincent,
*Mr Tope Popoola.
*University Press Plc Ibadan voluntarily served as
the secretariat for the forum and made available the part-time services of Mrs. L.L. Onifade as secretary.
The task force held nine meetings and was grante four hearings by the Presidential Advisory
Committee between June 1990 June 1991. After weighing the pros and cons of establishing a central
book development agency as a government parastatal, the Task Force opted for a non-
governmental, non-profit Foundation. On July 16, 1991, the Nigerian Book Development Forum met at
Ibadan and endorsed the establishment of the Nigerian Book Foundation (NBF), thereby
dissolving itself. A Board of Directors established on the same date ceased to exist in December 1992 on
technical grounds. A Board of Trustees, which replaced it held its first meeting in Lagos on April 15,
1993.
The Foundation set up its Secretariat at Awka, Anambra State, in October 1993, and in December
14, 1994, the National Advisory Council of the foundation held its first meeting in Lagos.
